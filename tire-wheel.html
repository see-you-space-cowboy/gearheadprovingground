Understanding Tire Sizes and Speedometer Accuracy

When you change tire sizes, your speedometer and odometer become inaccurate because they're calibrated for the original tire diameter. Larger tires make your speedometer read slower than actual speed, while smaller tires make it read faster. This affects not only your speed awareness but also your odometer mileage and fuel economy calculations.

How Tire Size Affects Speed

Your vehicle's speedometer calculates speed based on how fast the wheels are rotating. When you install larger diameter tires, each revolution covers more ground, so you're traveling faster than the speedometer indicates. For example, if you install tires that are 10% larger in diameter, your actual speed will be 10% higher than what your speedometer shows.

Common Tire Size Scenarios

Lifted Trucks: Installing larger tires on lifted trucks is common but can result in significant speedometer error. A typical lift with 35" tires replacing 31" tires creates about 13% speedometer error.

Plus-Sizing Wheels: When upgrading to larger diameter wheels, you typically use lower profile tires to maintain similar overall diameter. If done correctly, speedometer accuracy is maintained.

Performance Cars: Performance enthusiasts often install wider tires without changing overall diameter to maintain speedometer accuracy while gaining traction.

Classic Cars: Restorers may need to install modern tire sizes that differ from original equipment, requiring speedometer gear changes or electronic correction.

Legal and Safety Concerns

An inaccurate speedometer can lead to speeding tickets if you're traveling faster than you think. In some jurisdictions, an incorrect speedometer can fail vehicle inspection. Additionally, your odometer will record incorrect mileage, affecting resale value, warranty claims, and lease agreements.

Speedometer Correction Solutions

  • Mechanical Speedometers: Change the speedometer drive gear in the transmission to match new tire size
  • Electronic Speedometers: Reprogram the vehicle's ECU/PCM with a tuner or install a speedometer calibrator module
  • GPS Speedometers: Install an aftermarket GPS-based speedometer for perfect accuracy regardless of tire size
  • Gear Ratio Change: Changing gear ratios also requires speedometer recalibration
